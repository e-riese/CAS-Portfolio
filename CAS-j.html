×

Juvenile Horseshoe Crab Rearing Programme
馬蹄蟹校園保母計劃

Ocean Park Conservation Foundation (OPCFHK) runs a partnership with schools across Hong Kong where students can rear endangered juvenille horseshoe crabs until they are big enough for rehabilitation back into the wild. Our club would have students sign up for shifts on the weekend to look after them, which involved feeding, weighing and measuring the crabs. I learned a lot about how to take care of the creatures - like using a salinometer and how to handle them. Our crabs unfortunately didn't fare well and we ended up not having any from our club to release that day but members from our group still went to learn and participate. We were also invited to CityU where the main lab for the rehabilitation was to do a tour and get a small info session on the horseshoe crabs. They kept some of the bigger ones in blow up pools to study. This wasn't really in the IB years (it was over 2013) but I really enjoyed being part of this club.

Focus Week Cambodia Siem Reap

I volunteered to help build schools and homes for communities in need in Cambodia.

×

Focus Week Trip Cambodia Siem Reap

Each year our school has a Focus Week where students can either do an action, creative or service focused trip, and for certain years work experience is done instead. In 2012 I went on a service based trip to Cambodia where we were to help build schools and homes for impoverished communities. The organization used 'eco bricks' to build the schools. These were old plastic bottles washed out and refilled with trash then coated in concrete. The homes were built through traditional methods using bamboo. I remembered it rained so much and we got soaked mid through building the houses. We also did a small fun class with some of the students who would be using the school which was really great. We also got to see a few sights while we were there like Angkor Wat and Angkor Thom. It was a really amazing experience and trip overall.
